# Resident FAQ — Land2Living

**Who is this for:** Residents of the Ndebele Royal Council  
**Questions?** Ask the council secretary, or WhatsApp the Land2Living team

---

## What is Land2Living?

Land2Living is a digital system for the Ndebele Royal Council that records who lives where, keeps track of land allocations, and helps residents access services from verified local providers.

Think of it as a digital version of the council's land register — always up to date and accessible from your phone.

---

## What is a PTO?

A **Permission to Occupy (PTO)** is your official right to live on and use your stand. The council issues it to you. With Land2Living, your PTO is:

- Stored digitally and can never be lost or burned in a fire
- Signed with a cryptographic signature that proves it's genuine
- Printable as a PDF with a QR code anyone can scan to verify it

Your existing paper PTO remains valid — the digital PTO is in addition to it.

---

## Why is the council registering me digitally?

The Ndebele Royal Council wants to:
1. Protect your occupancy rights with a tamper-proof digital record
2. Make it faster to process land applications, transfers, and services
3. Create an official map of all occupied stands in the community

Your registration does not change your rights — it strengthens them.

---

## What information is stored about me?

- Your full name
- Your ID number
- Your phone number
- Your date of birth
- Your photo and a photo of your ID document
- Which stand you occupy and since when

---

## Is my information safe?

Yes. Land2Living is required to protect your information under the **Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA)**. Your data:

- Is stored on secure servers in South Africa
- Is encrypted (your ID number and personal details are not stored as plain text)
- Is only accessible to council staff with proper authorisation
- Can be requested or deleted by you (see below)

---

## Can I see my own information?

Yes. Contact the council secretary and ask for a copy of your data. We are required to provide it within 30 days.

---

## Can I ask for my information to be deleted?

Yes, with one exception. You can ask for your personal details to be removed. However, your PTO record and occupancy history cannot be deleted — they are official legal records of your right to the land, and removing them would harm you.

---

## What is a land application?

If you want to be allocated a new stand in the community, you submit a land application through the Land2Living app or on paper. The council secretary reviews it and decides to approve, defer, or reject it. If approved, a PTO is issued to you.

---

## What is the resale marketplace?

If you want to sell your stand or property, you can list it on the Land2Living marketplace. Other residents can make offers. The council secretary reviews and approves transfers. The digital PTO is updated to the new occupant when the transfer is complete.

---

## What are the services?

Land2Living has a list of verified local service providers — plumbers, electricians, builders, cleaners, gardeners, and more. You can book a service through the app:

1. Describe what you need
2. The provider submits a quote
3. You accept the quote
4. Work is done
5. You and the provider both confirm completion
6. Payment is released

For new relationships, the platform holds your payment safely until both sides confirm the work is done.

---

## How do I download the app?

1. Open **Chrome** on your phone
2. Go to `ndebele.land2living.co.za`
3. Log in (ask the council secretary for your login details)
4. Chrome will offer to add it to your home screen — accept this
5. The app will appear on your home screen

---

## Who do I contact if I have a problem?

Speak to the **council secretary** at the Ndebele Royal Council offices, or WhatsApp the Land2Living support number (ask the council secretary for this number).
